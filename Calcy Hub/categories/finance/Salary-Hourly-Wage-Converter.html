Understanding Salary vs Hourly

Salaried Employees receive a fixed annual amount regardless of hours worked. Benefits often include paid time off and health insurance.

Hourly Employees are paid for each hour worked. They typically receive overtime pay for hours beyond 40 per week.

Example: A $60,000/year salary equals approximately $28.85/hour based on 2,080 working hours per year (40 hrs/week √ó 52 weeks).

Key Factors to Consider

  • Benefits: Health insurance, retirement contributions
  • Overtime: Hourly workers may earn time-and-a-half
  • Stability: Salaried positions offer predictable income
  • Flexibility: Hourly work may offer variable schedules
  • Taxes: Both are subject to income tax and deductions
  • Paid Time Off: Usually more common for salaried roles
